Mist & Drip Irrigation System

Mist irrigation disperses water in the form of fine droplets, creating a humid environment that nourishes plants without over-saturating the soil. Drip irrigation, on the other hand, delivers water directly to the roots in a slow and controlled manner, reducing water waste and evaporation.

Irrigation Comparison

To compare the efficiency and effectiveness of misting and drip irrigation systems with an automated water tank refilling system in Basil Cultivation.

Specific Objectives

Water Usage

Monitoring and Comparing the Water Consumption of mist and drip irrigation systems. By utilizing IoT sensors, the project will track the amount of water used by each system during the basil cultivation process.

Energy Usage

Using IoT-enabled power sensors, the project will evaluate the electricity required for the operation of both systems. This data will allow for a comparison of the energy efficiency between the two methods.

Crop Yield

Analyzing the impact of the two irrigation systems on basil crop yield. The project will monitor the growth rate, and final yield of basil plants cultivated under mist and drip irrigation.

We Are FASCROP

FASCROP is a dedicated group of three 4th-year Computer Engineering students from the University of San Agustin - Iloilo, who are engaged in their thesis research project. Their research, titled "IrriGaia: IoT-Based Comparison on Mist and Drip Irrigation System for Basil Cultivation," focuses on leveraging IoT technology to analyze and compare the efficiency of two advanced irrigation systems in growing basil.

The group is guided by their research adviser, collaborating to explore sustainable agricultural practices through technology-driven solutions. FASCROP embodies a strong commitment to innovation, applying their engineering skills to address real-world challenges in precision agriculture.
